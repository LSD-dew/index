INTRODUCTION

Dengue is a major public health challenge in Sri Lanka. The Kelaniya MOH area is a high‑risk zone (Amarasinghe & Nadeeka, 2021).

Existing models provide district/MOH‑level predictions, lacking the GN‑level granularity needed for targeted interventions. Moreover, they often ignore non‑climatic drivers like population density and flood occurrence.

Our research fills this gap by developing a real‑time, fine‑grained dengue forecasting system at GN level, integrating weekly climate data, population, and flood occurrence (from our XGBoost flood model) to forecast dengue risk every week for all Grama Niladari divisions in Kelaniya Divisional Secretariat.

34 Grama Niladari divisions – each with weekly dengue risk forecast

OBJECTIVES

Main objective

Develop a weekly dengue forecasting system for each GN division in Kelaniya using LSTM that integrates historical & real‑time climate, population, and flood data.

Specific objectives

  • Identify key climate factors influencing dengue at GN level
  • Determine relationship between climate, population, flood & dengue
  • Build XGBoost flood prediction model (input for dengue system)
  • Develop LSTM model for weekly dengue cases per GN
  • Integrate real‑time data streams
  • Design interactive dashboard for health officials

RESEARCH QUESTIONS

Climate influencers? What climate factors most strongly influence dengue incidence at GN level?

Relationship? How do historical climate, population & flood relate to dengue at GN level?

Flood prediction? How effectively can XGBoost predict weekly flood occurrence using hydro‑meteorological data?

LSTM forecast? How can an LSTM model accurately forecast weekly dengue cases using input data?

Real‑time impact? How does integrating real‑time climate data affect accuracy & timeliness?

Dashboard? How to design an interactive dashboard that communicates risk & supports decisions?

METHODOLOGY

1 Flood (XGBoost)

Inputs: rainfall, soil moisture, slope, water level, historical floods 2015–25.
Output: weekly flood occurrence & duration → dengue DB.

2 Dengue (LSTM)

Inputs: weekly cases, climate, population, flood, 4‑week lags.
Output: GN‑level dengue risk (weekly).
